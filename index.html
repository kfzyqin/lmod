üëãIntroduction

This study proposes a systematic and reproducible data and evaluation pipeline that repurposes existing datasets to curate a dataset we refer to as LMOD (Large Multimodal Ophthalmology Dataset) for the development and evaluation of LVLMs in ophthalmology.

  1. LMOD consists of five ophthalmic imaging modalities: OCT, CFP, scanning laser ophthalmoscopy (SLO), lens photographs, and surgical scenes, along with biomarkers and free text, collectively comprising over 20K
  2. LMOD provides multi-granular annotations, including bounding boxes, region annotations, and image-level annotations. The pipeline can be directly applied to new datasets.
  3. We further systematically assess 13 state-of-the-art LVLMs on (1) anatomical recognition, which quantifies the effectiveness of LVLMs at the region level. (2) disease diagnosis and classification, which quantifies the effectiveness of LVLMs for ophthalmic diseases. (3) demographic information extraction to assess whether LVLMs can infer demographic variables such as age and gender from ophthalmic images

üèÜFull Anatomical Recognition Results

We evaluate state-of-the-art large multimodal models (LMMs) with proposed metrics, including proprietary and open-source models. Our evaluation is conducted under a zero-shot setting to assess the capability of LMMs to generate accurate marked-region annotations. For all models, we use the same prompt template for each ophthalmology dataset. For evaluation, we compare the model's output with the ground truth annotations using strict string matching. A predicted label is considered correct only if it exactly matches the corresponding ground truth label, resulting in a binary score of 1 for a perfect match and 0 for a mismatch.
